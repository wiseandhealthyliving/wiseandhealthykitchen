What To Reject

Materials to Avoid in Air Fryers

Teflon (PTFE) Coatings

Releases toxic PFAS compounds above 500Â°F. Air fryers can create hot spots exceeding this temperature, especially in cheaper models with uneven heat distribution.

Polycarbonate Plastic

Contains BPA. Releases hormone-disrupting chemicals when heated above 200Â°F â€” well below air fryer operating temperatures. Common in budget air fryer windows and housings.

PFOA-Containing Non-Stick

A known carcinogen, banned in cookware in many countries since 2015. Still found in some imported products. Any non-stick coating should be explicitly labeled PFOA-free.

Vague "Non-Stick" Claims

Any air fryer marketed simply as "non-stick" without specifying the coating type should be treated with suspicion. Reputable manufacturers disclose materials. If they won't, walk away.
